Pedagogical Articles

Over the years, I have done extensive research in the field of musicianship and musical pedagogy. I have written about my findings in hopes to teach and inspire my peers. I have found that many of the traditional pedagogical approaches to music education are outdated and are not conducive towards a greater connectivity with music. My writings discuss some of the modern approaches that I take in the realm of bettering the relationship between the musician and their instrument.

Meditative Musicianship

How to Utilize Meditation to Further Musicianship

Meditation has many applications in music. I started doing research on this matter to alleviate performance anxiety. What I found is that the relief of anxiety is just the tip of the iceberg when discussing the connection between music and meditation. I am student of the teaching of Kenny Werner and Effortless Mastery, and have taken his ideas and expanded upon them to further fit my needs in musicianship. There are many purposes of combining meditation and music, but i found that for me, the most important aspect of the combination is to feel unconditional love for myself and the music that I create.

Compositional Improvisation

Anyone can learn language... How do you turn that language into POETRY?

Music pedagogy has a gaping hole that leaves many musicians stranded and anxious. I have found that many teachers heavily involve their students in the learning of language. What they neglect to do is teach them the ways that the language can be utilized. Language should not be taught before the utilization of said language. The two should be taught in tandem. That is like learning every word in the english language but not knowing how to formulate a sentence. The papers below show how to utilize language. Many of these papers are not just my own theories, but deep analysis of the greats, including John Coltrane and Pat Metheny.
